The Quiet Power of Deep Work
in the Age of AI

人工智能时代下深度工作的静默力量

In a world of constant distraction, the ability to focus may become the ultimate competitive advantage.

Briefing By the Editorial Board April 2025 · 6 min read

In an age dominated by artificial intelligence and endless notifications, the capacity for deep, uninterrupted work has become increasingly rare—and therefore increasingly valuable. While many fear that AI will replace human labour, a more subtle threat is emerging: the erosion of our ability to think deeply.

在人工智能与无尽通知主导的时代，进行深度、无干扰工作的能力变得越来越稀缺，因此也越来越有价值。虽然许多人担心人工智能会取代人类劳动，但一种更微妙的威胁正在浮现：我们深度思考能力的逐渐侵蚀。

The concept of “deep work”, popularised by computer scientist Cal Newport, refers to the ability to focus without distraction on cognitively demanding tasks. This skill is not merely about productivity; it is about creating work that is meaningful, original and difficult to replicate. In contrast, “shallow work”—answering emails, attending back-to-back meetings, scrolling through updates—can be performed while distracted and is increasingly being automated.

由计算机科学家 Cal Newport 提出的深度工作概念，指的是在无干扰状态下专注于认知要求高的任务的能力。这种技能不仅关乎生产力，更关乎创造有意义、原创且难以复制的工作。相比之下，浅层工作——回复邮件、参加连续会议、刷新动态——可以在分心状态下完成，并且正越来越多地被自动化。

Research suggests that the human brain is not wired for sustained attention. Every time we switch tasks, we incur a “switch cost” in both time and cognitive energy. Over time, this constant fragmentation weakens our ability to engage in the kind of complex, creative thinking that AI cannot easily replicate. The irony is that as AI becomes more powerful, the value of human deep work rises.

研究表明，人脑并不为持续注意力而生。每当我们切换任务，都会在时间与认知能量上产生切换成本。随着时间推移，这种持续的碎片化会削弱我们进行复杂、创造性思考的能力，而这正是人工智能难以轻易复制的。讽刺的是，随着人工智能日益强大，人类深度工作的价值反而上升。
