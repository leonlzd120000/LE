Decoder podcast discussion on AI, music, and award rules

AI is blowing up music. How should the Grammys handle it?

AI 正在重塑音乐：格莱美该如何应对？

A self-contained English-learning package about how generative AI is changing music production, why the Recording Academy is focused on human creativity, and how award rules may evolve as AI-made songs flood streaming platforms.

来源 The Verge 原文链接

Generative AI has moved from a side topic to a central issue in music. Producers, songwriters, and platforms now face a new question: when a machine helps make a song, who should receive credit?

生成式 AI 已经从边缘话题变成音乐行业的核心问题。制作人、词曲作者和平台现在都要面对一个新问题：当机器帮助创作歌曲时，谁应该获得署名？

The Verge's Decoder episode focuses on Harvey Mason Jr., the CEO of the Recording Academy, the organization behind the Grammy Awards. His role is complicated because he is both an industry leader and an experienced music creator.

The Verge 的 Decoder 节目聚焦于 Harvey Mason Jr.，他是负责格莱美奖的 Recording Academy 首席执行官。他的角色很复杂，因为他既是行业管理者，也是一位经验丰富的音乐创作者。

AI tools can now suggest melodies, create backing vocals, generate stems, and help musicians test ideas quickly. For some artists, AI acts like a tireless writing partner that offers many directions in seconds.

AI 工具现在可以提出旋律建议、生成和声、制作分轨素材，并帮助音乐人快速测试想法。对一些艺术家来说，AI 就像一个不知疲倦的写作伙伴，能在几秒内提供许多方向。

The challenge is not only quality, but also disclosure. If a song sounds human but was partly generated by software, award committees, fans, and streaming platforms may all want clearer information.

挑战不仅在于质量，也在于透明披露。如果一首歌听起来像真人创作，但部分由软件生成，那么评奖委员会、歌迷和流媒体平台都可能希望获得更清晰的信息。

The Recording Academy's current approach is not a simple ban on every AI-assisted track. Instead, the focus is whether meaningful human creativity is present in the submitted work.

Recording Academy 目前的做法并不是简单禁止所有使用 AI 辅助的作品。它关注的是参评作品中是否存在有意义的人类创造力。

This creates difficult category questions. If AI performs a vocal part, that may affect a performance award. If a human singer delivers an AI-written song with skill and emotion, the judgment may be different.

这会带来复杂的奖项类别问题。如果 AI 完成了演唱部分，可能会影响表演类奖项；如果真人歌手用技巧和情感演唱了一首 AI 写的歌，判断结果可能不同。

Streaming services are seeing a rapid rise in AI-generated tracks. This growth can make discovery harder, because listeners and platforms must separate serious creative work from mass-produced audio content.

流媒体服务正在看到 AI 生成歌曲的快速增长。这种增长会让发现好音乐变得更难，因为听众和平台必须区分严肃创作和大规模生产的音频内容。

Mason's argument points to a larger cultural tension. Technology can expand what artists can make, but music still matters because it reflects lived experience, emotion, collaboration, and human judgment.

Mason 的观点指向一种更大的文化张力。技术可以扩展艺术家的创作能力，但音乐之所以重要，仍然是因为它反映了生活经验、情感、协作和人类判断。

The industry may eventually treat some AI tools like drum machines or autotune: controversial at first, then normal. But the rules around consent, training data, credit, and awards are still unsettled.

行业最终可能会像看待鼓机或自动调音一样看待某些 AI 工具：一开始有争议，后来变得正常。但关于授权、训练数据、署名和奖项的规则仍未稳定。

For English learners, this topic is useful because it combines technology, law, art, and business. It also shows how one word, such as creativity, can carry both practical and emotional meaning.

对英语学习者来说，这个话题很有价值，因为它结合了技术、法律、艺术和商业。它也展示了一个词，比如 creativity，如何同时承载实际意义和情感意义。
